## Key Voice Characteristics

### Core Principles
1. **Structured Brevity** - Concise LinkedIn posts, frameworks for longer content
2. **Authentic Expertise** - Share real experiences, admit limitations
3. **Actionable Frameworks** - Provide structured tools readers can use
4. **Contrarian Clarity** - Challenge assumptions when backed by research
5. **Research-Informed Insights** - Data-driven perspectives, transparent methodology

### Tone
- Confident but humble
- Practical over theoretical
- Conversational but professional
- Direct and clear
- Research-backed

### Personality
- Curious learner
- Practical problem-solver
- Honest about trade-offs
- Framework-oriented
- Community-focused

## Content Structure

### LinkedIn Posts (50-150 words)
1. Hook (contrarian, surprising, or problem)
2. Core insight
3. Quick context (if needed)
4. Actionable takeaway
5. Optional engagement question

### Medium Posts (300-800 words)
1. Problem statement
2. Why it matters
3. Research/evidence
4. Framework (3-7 steps)
5. Implementation guide
6. Key takeaways

### Long-Form (1000-2000 words)
1. Executive summary
2. Problem definition
3. Research methodology
4. Findings
5. Framework
6. Implementation guide
7. Best practices
8. Conclusion

## Language Patterns

### Opening Hooks
- "Here's what I've learned about [topic]..."
- "After researching [X], I found..."
- "The problem with [common approach] is..."
- "Most people think [X], but here's what actually works..."

### Framework Introductions
- "Here's a [X]-step framework I've developed..."
- "After analyzing [research], here's the structure that works..."
- "Based on my research, here's how to approach this..."

### Personal Insights
- "What I've learned is..."
- "My research shows..."
- "I've found that..."
- "Here's what actually works in practice..."

## Formatting Guidelines

### LinkedIn Posts
- Minimal formatting (plain text preferred)
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Line breaks for readability
- No excessive emojis
- Scannable format

### Medium Posts
- Clear headings
- Numbered lists for frameworks
- Bullet points for takeaways
- Bold for key concepts (sparingly)
- White space for readability

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's ✅
- Keep LinkedIn posts concise (50-150 words)
- Provide frameworks readers can use immediately
- Share research methodology and findings
- Be honest about limitations and trade-offs
- Use structured approaches (numbered steps)
- Connect everything to business outcomes
- Challenge assumptions when backed by research
- Show your work and thinking process

### Don'ts ❌
- Over-explain or add unnecessary words
- Use jargon without explanation
- Pretend to know everything
- Create frameworks without practical application
- Ignore research or data
- Use corporate speak or marketing fluff
- Skip the "why" behind recommendations
- Make claims without evidence

## Content Length Guidelines

- **LinkedIn Posts:** 50-150 words (sweet spot: 80-120)
- **Medium Posts:** 300-800 words
- **Long-Form:** 1000-2000+ words
