# Personal Writing Voice & Style Guide

**Created:** December 30, 2025  
**Inspired by:** Allie K. Miller, Dharmesh Shah, and personal writing style  
**Purpose:** Define a unique, effective writing voice that combines the best elements from successful influencers

---

## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Structured Brevity
- **Ultra-concise LinkedIn posts** (50-150 words) - Inspired by Dharmesh's brevity
- **Frameworks for longer content** - Inspired by Allie's structured approach
- **Every word earns its place** - No fluff, maximum impact
- **Scannable format** - Easy to digest quickly

### 2. Authentic Expertise
- **Share real experiences** - What you've actually learned and done
- **Admit limitations** - Honest about what you don't know (Dharmesh's authenticity)
- **Show your work** - Research methodology and process (your strength)
- **No corporate speak** - Genuine voice, not marketing fluff

### 3. Actionable Frameworks
- **Provide structured tools** - Inspired by Allie's frameworks
- **Make it immediately usable** - Readers can apply today
- **Numbered steps** - Clear, scannable format
- **Real-world examples** - Connect to actual use cases

### 4. Contrarian Clarity
- **Challenge assumptions** - When you have a good reason (Dharmesh's approach)
- **Back it up with research** - Your strength in methodology
- **Make complex simple** - Break down sophisticated concepts (Allie's clarity)
- **Business-focused** - Always connect to outcomes

### 5. Research-Informed Insights
- **Data-driven perspectives** - Your research methodology strength
- **Multi-source validation** - Cross-reference and verify
- **Show your thinking** - Transparent about how you reached conclusions
- **Practical application** - Connect research to real-world use

## Voice Characteristics

### Tone
- **Confident but humble** - Share expertise without arrogance
- **Practical over theoretical** - Focus on what works
- **Conversational but professional** - Approachable, not casual
- **Direct and clear** - Get to the point quickly
- **Research-backed** - Support claims with evidence

### Personality
- **Curious learner** - Always exploring and researching
- **Practical problem-solver** - Focus on solutions, not just problems
- **Honest about trade-offs** - Don't sugarcoat realities
- **Framework-oriented** - Love creating structured approaches
- **Community-focused** - Share learnings openly

## Content Structure Patterns

### LinkedIn Posts (Ultra-Short Form: 50-150 words)

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** - Contrarian statement, surprising insight, or direct problem
2. **Core insight** - One key point or framework
3. **Quick context** - 1-2 sentences if needed
4. **Actionable takeaway** - What readers can do
5. **Optional engagement** - Subtle question or reflection

**Example Pattern:**
```
[Contrarian statement or surprising insight]

Here's what I've learned: [Core insight]

[Brief context or example]

[Actionable takeaway or framework]

[Optional: What's your experience with this?]
```

### Medium Posts (Structured Frameworks: 300-800 words)

**Structure:**
1. **Problem statement** - What's wrong or missing
2. **Why it matters** - Business impact or consequences
3. **Research/evidence** - What you've found or learned
4. **Framework** - Structured solution (3-7 steps)
5. **Implementation** - How to apply it
6. **Key takeaways** - Summary points

**Example Pattern:**
```
[Problem: What's the issue?]

[Why this matters - Business impact]

After researching [topic], here's what I found: [Key insights]

Here's a [X]-step framework:
1. [Step with context]
2. [Step with context]
3. [Step with context]

[How to implement]

Key takeaways:
- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]
```

### Long-Form Content (Comprehensive Guides: 1000-2000 words)

**Structure:**
1. **Executive summary** - Key points upfront
2. **Problem definition** - Detailed context
3. **Research methodology** - How you approached it
4. **Findings** - What you discovered
5. **Framework** - Structured solution
6. **Implementation guide** - Step-by-step
7. **Best practices** - Do's and don'ts
8. **Conclusion** - Summary and next steps

## Language Patterns

### Opening Hooks
- "Here's what I've learned about [topic]..."
- "After researching [X], I found..."
- "The problem with [common approach] is..."
- "Most people think [X], but here's what actually works..."
- "I've been exploring [topic] and discovered..."

### Framework Introductions
- "Here's a [X]-step framework I've developed..."
- "After analyzing [research], here's the structure that works..."
- "Based on my research, here's how to approach this..."
- "I've broken this down into [X] key steps..."

### Personal Insights
- "What I've learned is..."
- "My research shows..."
- "I've found that..."
- "Here's what actually works in practice..."

### Contrarian Statements
- "Everyone says [X], but here's what the research shows..."
- "The common advice is [X], but I've found..."
- "Most people focus on [X], but [Y] is what actually matters..."

### Closing Patterns
- "What's your experience with [topic]?"
- "Which step resonates most with you?"
- "Here's what to try next..."
- "The key takeaway: [summary]"

## Formatting Guidelines

### LinkedIn Posts
- **Minimal formatting** - Plain text preferred
- **Short paragraphs** - 1-2 sentences max
- **Line breaks** - For readability, not decoration
- **No excessive emojis** - Use sparingly if at all
- **Scannable** - Easy to read quickly

### Medium Posts
- **Clear headings** - Use for structure
- **Numbered lists** - For frameworks and steps
- **Bullet points** - For takeaways and features
- **Bold** - For key concepts (sparingly)
- **White space** - For readability

### Long-Form Content
- **Executive summary** - Upfront
- **Clear sections** - Well-organized
- **Visual hierarchy** - Headings, subheadings
- **Research citations** - When applicable
- **Actionable takeaways** - Highlighted

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's ✅
- ✅ Keep LinkedIn posts concise (50-150 words)
- ✅ Provide frameworks readers can use immediately
- ✅ Share research methodology and findings
- ✅ Be honest about limitations and trade-offs
- ✅ Use structured approaches (numbered steps)
- ✅ Connect everything to business outcomes
- ✅ Challenge assumptions when backed by research
- ✅ Show your work and thinking process
- ✅ Make complex topics accessible
- ✅ Post consistently

### Don'ts ❌
- ❌ Over-explain or add unnecessary words
- ❌ Use jargon without explanation
- ❌ Pretend to know everything
- ❌ Create frameworks without practical application
- ❌ Ignore research or data
- ❌ Use corporate speak or marketing fluff
- ❌ Skip the "why" behind recommendations
- ❌ Make claims without evidence
- ❌ Over-format posts
- ❌ Post inconsistently

## Framework Development Approach

### Creating Effective Frameworks
1. **Identify the problem** - What does this solve?
2. **Research the topic** - What does data/research show?
3. **Break into steps** - 3-7 clear, actionable steps
4. **Provide context** - Why each step matters
5. **Show application** - How to use it
6. **Validate** - Test with real examples

### Framework Structure Template
```
[Framework Name]

Problem: [What this solves]

Research shows: [Key findings]

Here's a [X]-step framework:

1. [Step name]
   - What to do: [Action]
   - Why it matters: [Context]
   - Example: [Real-world application]

2. [Step name]
   - What to do: [Action]
   - Why it matters: [Context]
   - Example: [Real-world application]

3. [Step name]
   - What to do: [Action]
   - Why it matters: [Context]
   - Example: [Real-world application]

Implementation: [How to apply]

Key takeaways:
- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]
```

## Posting Strategy

### Consistency
- **LinkedIn:** 3-5 times per week
- **Medium posts:** 1-2 times per week
- **Long-form:** Monthly or as topics warrant
- **Consistent voice** across all platforms

### Content Mix
- **60% frameworks** - Structured, actionable content
- **30% research insights** - Data-driven perspectives
- **10% personal reflections** - Learnings and experiences

### Timing
- **LinkedIn:** Morning posts (8-10 AM) for visibility
- **Medium posts:** Mid-week for engagement
- **Long-form:** When you have comprehensive content
