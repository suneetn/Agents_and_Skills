## Pre-Trip Essentials

### Before You Arrive
- **Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC):** Fill out online before arrival (mandatory as of May 2025)
- **SIM Card:** Get a local SIM or eSIM for data (essential for navigation and Grab app)
- **Apps to Download:**
  - **Grab** - For taxis and food delivery
  - **Bolt** - Alternative ride-hailing app
  - **Google Maps** or **Google Translate**
  - **BTS Skytrain** app for route planning

### Getting Around
- **BTS Skytrain & MRT:** Best for avoiding traffic, ~$4 USD for one-day pass
- **Tuk-tuks:** Fun experience but negotiate price first (usually 100-200 baht for short trips)
- **River Ferry:** Scenic way to travel along Chao Phraya River (15-40 baht)
- **Grab/Bolt:** Convenient for longer distances or when tired

### Money Tips
- Carry cash (many places don't accept cards)
- Use smaller bills (20, 50, 100 baht) - easier for street vendors
- ATMs available everywhere (withdrawal fee ~220 baht)
- Exchange money at SuperRich (better rates than airport)

## Day 1: Markets, Temples & Old Bangkok

### Early Morning (6:00 AM - 8:00 AM)

**6:00 AM - Chatuchak Weekend Market** ‚≠ê *Sunday Only - Critical Timing*
- **Transportation from The Ritz-Carlton:**
  - **BTS:** Ratchadamri ‚Üí Siam ‚Üí transfer to Sukhumvit Line ‚Üí Mo Chit (25-30 min, 44 baht)
  - **Or:** Grab/Taxi directly (20-30 min, 150-250 baht depending on traffic)
- **Opening Hours:** 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (best to visit early morning to avoid heat and crowds)
- **Entry Fee:** FREE
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Why Visit:** Bangkok's iconic mega market with more than 10,000 stalls - one of the world's largest markets
- **Highlights:**
  - Sprawling open-air labyrinth selling everything
  - Clothing, accessories, home decor, art, antiques, vintage finds
  - Plants, artwork, and every kind of street food imaginable
  - Great for souvenirs and unique finds
  - Perfect for family members of all ages
- **Tips:**
  - Arrive early (before 8:00 AM) for best experience - avoids heat and crowds
  - Wear comfortable shoes (lots of walking)
  - Bring cash (smaller bills for bargaining)
  - Stay hydrated (many food/drink vendors throughout)
  - Bargain politely (expected at markets)
  - Sample street food for breakfast while exploring
- **Must-Try:** Fresh coconut water, grilled meats, Thai desserts, fresh tropical fruits
- **Afterwards:** Take a break at the massive nearby park (can rent a bike for just over a dollar)

**7:30 AM - Departure from Chatuchak to Grand Palace Area**
- **Transportation to Grand Palace:**
  - **Option 1 (Recommended):** BTS from Mo Chit ‚Üí Siam ‚Üí transfer to Silom Line ‚Üí Saphan Taksin (25 min, 44 baht)
    - Then: Walk to Sathorn Pier ‚Üí Take Chao Phraya Express Boat (Orange flag) ‚Üí Tha Chang Pier (15 min, 15 baht)
    - Walk 5 minutes to Grand Palace
  - **Option 2:** Grab/Taxi directly from Chatuchak (30-45 min depending on traffic, 200-300 baht)
  - **Option 3:** Hotel concierge can arrange private car (more expensive but comfortable for family)
- **Total Travel Time:** 40-50 minutes via BTS+Ferry, or 30-45 min by car

**8:00 AM - Breakfast**
- **Location:** Home Cafe Tatienne (near Grand Palace) or local eatery

- **Cost:** 50-100 baht per person

### Morning (8:30 AM - 12:00 PM)

**8:30 AM - Grand Palace & Temple of the Emerald Buddha (Wat Phra Kaew)**
- **Opening:** 8:30 AM (arrive early to avoid crowds!)
- **Entry Fee:** 500 baht (~$14 USD) per person
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Why Visit:** Former royal residence established in 1782, ceremonial home of Thailand's monarchs for over two centuries. One of the country's most important cultural landmarks and Bangkok's most famous attraction.
- **Dress Code:** 
  - ‚úÖ Long pants/skirt (below knees)
  - ‚úÖ Shoulders covered
  - ‚úÖ No tight clothing
  - ‚úÖ Sandals OK (but bring socks just in case)
- **Highlights:**
  - Temple of the Emerald Buddha (Wat Phra Kaew) - centerpiece carved from single piece of jade
  - Intricate architecture and colorful buildings
  - Royal residences and ceremonial halls
  - Breathtaking ornate structures throughout the complex
  - Take your time - there's much to see
- **‚ö†Ô∏è Scam Alert:** Ignore anyone saying the palace is "closed" - they're trying to redirect you to shops. The palace is open daily.

**11:00 AM - Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha)**
- **Location:** 10-minute walk from Grand Palace
- **Entry Fee:** 200 baht (~$6 USD)
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Opening Hours:** 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
- **Why Visit:** Home to the massive 46-meter long Reclining Buddha and considered the birthplace of traditional Thai massage
- **Highlights:**
  - Massive 46-meter long Reclining Buddha (gold-plated statue)
  - Beautiful temple grounds with Chinese statues
  - Peaceful courtyards, intricate murals, countless chedis wrapped in colorful ceramic tiles
  - Traditional Thai massage school (optional: 30-min massage for 420 baht / $12)
  - Grounds stretch far beyond the famous statue - explore the peaceful areas
- **Family Tip:** Great photo opportunities and the massage can be a nice break for tired feet

## Day 2: Floating Market, Culture & Modern Bangkok

### Early Morning (6:00 AM - 1:00 PM)

**6:00 AM - Early Departure for Damnoen Saduak Floating Market**
- **Location:** 1.5 hours drive from Bangkok city center (100 km southwest)
- **Transportation from The Ritz-Carlton:**
  - **Option 1 (Recommended for Family):** Book tour through hotel concierge (includes pickup/dropoff)
  - **Option 2:** Private car through hotel concierge (~3,000-4,000 baht round trip for 4 people)
  - **Option 3:** Grab/Taxi (negotiate round trip, ~2,500-3,500 baht)
  - **Option 4:** Public bus - BTS to Mo Chit ‚Üí Taxi to Southern Bus Terminal (Sai Tai Mai) ‚Üí Bus to Damnoen Saduak (80 baht, 2 hours) - **Not recommended for family**
- **Tour Options:** 
  - Half-day tours (usually 6:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
  - Often combined with Maeklong Railway Market
  - Cost: ~1,500-2,500 baht per person (includes transport)
- **Hotel Concierge Tip:** Ask them to arrange - they know reliable tour operators
- **Opening:** 7:00 AM - 12:00 PM (best to arrive early!)
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours including travel
- **Why Visit:** Traditional floating market experience - vendors selling from boats on canals. Unique cultural experience, though can be touristy.
- **Highlights:**
  - Traditional floating market experience
  - Vendors selling from boats on canals
  - Fresh fruits, vegetables, and local foods
  - Handcrafted souvenirs
  - Boat rides through the market (200-500 baht)
  - Authentic Thai cultural experience
- **Must-Try Foods:**
  - Fresh coconut water
  - Grilled seafood from boats
  - Pad Thai cooked on boats
  - Fresh tropical fruits
  - Thai desserts
- **Tips:**
  - Arrive early (before 8:00 AM) to avoid crowds
  - Bring cash (smaller bills)
  - Wear comfortable, waterproof shoes
  - Bargain for boat rides and souvenirs
  - Bring camera/phone for photos
  - Sunscreen and hat recommended
- **Research Note:** Some sources note prices can be higher than other markets, but you're paying for the unique experience

**Optional Addition: Maeklong Railway Market**
- **Location:** 15 minutes from Damnoen Saduak
- **Highlights:**
  - Market stalls on railway tracks
  - Trains pass through 8 times per day
  - Vendors quickly move goods when train approaches
  - Unique photo opportunity
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Best Time:** Check train schedule (usually around 8:30 AM, 11:10 AM, 2:30 PM)
- **Family Tip:** Exciting for all ages - seeing vendors move everything quickly is quite a spectacle

### Cultural Etiquette

- **Temples:** Remove shoes, cover knees and shoulders, be respectful
- **Feet:** Don't point feet at people or Buddha images
- **Head:** Don't touch people's heads (considered sacred)
- **Wai:** Traditional greeting (hands together, slight bow)
- **Bargaining:** OK at markets, be polite
- **Tipping:** Not mandatory but appreciated (10% at restaurants)
