## Executive Summary

Bangkok's culinary scene is renowned for its diverse offerings, ranging from traditional street food to high-end fine dining establishments. The city has become a global food destination, with the 2025 MICHELIN Guide Bangkok showcasing 462 dining venues, including Thailand's first-ever Three MICHELIN Star restaurant (Sorn), 7 Two MICHELIN Stars, 28 One MICHELIN Star, 156 Bib Gourmand, and 270 MICHELIN Selected restaurants.

This research synthesizes information from official Michelin Guide sources, travel blogs, and video content to provide a comprehensive guide to Bangkok's best restaurants across different categories and price points.

## Findings by Category

### Fine Dining & Michelin-Starred Restaurants

#### Three MICHELIN Stars (Highest Rating)

**Sorn**
- **Cuisine:** Southern Thai cuisine
- **Location:** Khlong Toei (restored mansion)
- **Highlights:**
  - First restaurant in Thailand to receive three Michelin stars (2025)
  - Emphasizes traditional techniques
  - Sources nearly all ingredients from Southern Thailand
  - Located in a beautifully restored mansion
- **Price Range:** Premium (fine dining)
- **Source:** Michelin Guide Thailand 2025

#### Two MICHELIN Stars

**Gaggan**
- **Cuisine:** Indian fusion (22-course multisensory experience)
- **Chef:** Gaggan Anand
- **Highlights:**
  - Ranked #6 in The World's 50 Best Restaurants list (2025)
  - #1 Best Restaurant in Asia (2025)
  - 22-course multisensory dining experience
  - Innovative Indian fusion cuisine
- **Price Range:** Premium (fine dining)
- **Source:** The World's 50 Best Restaurants, Michelin Guide

**Le Normandie**
- **Cuisine:** Contemporary French
- **Location:** Mandarin Oriental Hotel
- **Highlights:**
  - Panoramic views of the Chao Phraya River
  - Established in 1958
  - Chef de cuisine: Arnaud Dunand Sauthier
  - True Parisienne ambience
  - Five-star service
- **Price Range:** Premium (fine dining)
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Mezzaluna**
- **Cuisine:** European with Japanese precision
- **Location:** 65th floor of Lebua Hotel
- **Highlights:**
  - Sky-high panoramic views of Bangkok
  - Chef Ryuki Kawasaki (specializes in French cuisine)
  - Five- and seven-course set menus
  - Uses Japanese ingredients from chef's hometown
  - Extensive wine list
- **Price Range:** More than THB 2,400 ($76) per person
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

#### One MICHELIN Star & Michelin-Recommended Thai Restaurants

**Nahm**
- **Cuisine:** Sophisticated Thai cuisine
- **Location:** COMO Metropolitan Hotel
- **Chef:** David Thompson (Australian-born, highly experienced)
- **Highlights:**
  - Renowned worldwide for sophisticated Thai cuisine
  - Elevates traditional Thai cookery
  - Intense yet perfectly balanced flavors
  - Set menu offers surprising value
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Paste**
- **Cuisine:** Royal Thai cuisine
- **Highlights:**
  - Striking interior with spiral sculpture made from silk cocoons
  - Uses century-old cooking techniques
  - Ingredients sourced directly from local growers
  - Signature dishes: roast duck with nutmeg and coriander; fragrant hot and sour soup with crispy pork leg; yellow curry from the Gulf of Thailand
  - Attentive but not overbearing service
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Bo.lan**
- **Cuisine:** Royal Thai dishes
- **Chefs:** Duangporn Songvisava (Bo) and Dylan Jones (lan)
- **Highlights:**
  - Serving royal Thai dishes for nearly a decade
  - Heritage respected with modern presentation
  - Zero-carbon goal
  - Three degustation menus available
  - Attractive and intimate villa setting
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Chim by Siam Wisdom**
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Thai with modern influences
- **Highlights:**
  - Revisits traditional Thai recipes
  - Balance between old and new, Thai and foreign influences
  - À la carte or fixed price menu
  - Unmissable dishes: ancient tom yum with river fish and freshly made curries
  - Renovated in 2017
- **Price Range:** Moderate
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Saneh Jaan**
- **Cuisine:** Ancient royal recipes
- **Highlights:**
  - Thai dishes crafted from ancient recipes that once impressed the royal family
  - Vaulted ceilings and contemporary Thai touches
  - Mix of classics and hard-to-find recipes
  - Don't miss dessert: khao moa rang (rare creation with young green rice grains)
- **Price Range:** Moderate
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Sra Bua by Kiin Kiin**
- **Cuisine:** Modern innovative Thai
- **Highlights:**
  - One of the original purveyors of modern and innovative Thai cuisine
  - "The Journey" - 10-course menu showcasing full repertoire
  - Respects traditional Thai flavors but flips the script creatively
  - Inspired by Copenhagen's Kiin Kiin
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Jay Fai**
- **Cuisine:** Street food (Michelin-starred)
- **Highlights:**
  - Local legend wearing signature goggles
  - Continues what her father started 70 years ago
  - Famous for crab omelettes, crab curries, and dry congee
  - Both taxi drivers and foodies recommend
  - Unique: Michelin-starred street food vendor
- **Price Range:** Affordable (street food prices)
- **Source:** Michelin Guide, travel blogs

#### Innovative & Modern Restaurants

**Gaa**
- **Cuisine:** Modern eclectic (blends traditional cooking with innovative techniques)
- **Highlights:**
  - Locally sourced ingredients transformed into something unexpected
  - 10- and 14-course tasting menus (changed quarterly)
  - Signature items: chicken liver mousse and cauliflower with caramelized whey
  - Wine and juice pairings available
- **Price Range:** More than THB 2,400 ($76) per person
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Upstairs at Mikkeller**
- **Cuisine:** Innovative set menu
- **Location:** Second floor of popular beer bar (20-seater restaurant)
- **Highlights:**
  - Run by experienced but young chef (cut his teeth in Chicago)
  - 10-course set menu
  - Minimalist décor focusing on open kitchen and artful dishes
  - Menu changes seasonally
  - Accompanying beer pairing (brews from Denmark, New Zealand, USA)
- **Price Range:** More than THB 2,400 ($76) per person
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**La Table de Tee**
- **Cuisine:** Franco-Thai inspired
- **Location:** Back alley in Silom
- **Highlights:**
  - Bangkok-born chef with years of experience in Europe
  - Fixed five-course menu changes weekly
  - Locally sourced ingredients
  - Hybrid of creative French cuisine with Thai touches
  - Reasonably priced chef's menu
  - Advanced booking recommended
- **Price Range:** THB 800 to 1,600 ($25 to $51) per person
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**The Dining Room at The House on Sathorn**
- **Cuisine:** Asian with Turkish twist
- **Location:** Beautiful colonial mansion (dates from 1889, once Soviet Union embassy)
- **Highlights:**
  - Worth arriving early to wander around the mansion
  - Sumptuous bar perfect for cocktails
  - Ambitious combinations, flamboyant presentation
  - Turkish chef's travels inform his cuisine
  - Speciality: 'Sultan's Message Can't Deliver'
- **Price Range:** THB 1,600 to 2,400 ($51 to $76) per person
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

### Restaurants with Stunning Views

**Le Normandie** (see Two Stars section above)
- River views from Mandarin Oriental Hotel

**Mezzaluna** (see Two Stars section above)
- 65th floor panoramic city and river views

**La VIE**
- **Location:** 11th floor of VIE Hotel Bangkok (city center)
- **Cuisine:** Contemporary French-Japanese
- **Highlights:**
  - View complemented by cuisine
  - Dishes inspired by guest chefs from Michelin-starred restaurants
  - Fresh ingredients from local farmers' markets and Japan/France
  - Herbs and vegetables grown in hotel's rooftop garden
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Supanniga Eating Room (Tha Tian)**
- **Location:** Riverfront opposite Temple of Dawn
- **Cuisine:** Authentic Thai flavors
- **Highlights:**
  - Catch warm rays of setting sun
  - Signature dishes inspired by owner's grandmother's recipes
  - Special: moo ca muang (stewed pork with herbs and cha muang leaves)
  - Optional: Evening Supanniga River Cruise with gourmet meal
  - Or: Sip Champagne during cruise, then dine at restaurant
- **Price Range:** Moderate
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

**Scarlett**
- **Location:** 37th floor of Pullman Bangkok Hotel G (Silom)
- **Cuisine:** French and European traditional dishes
- **Highlights:**
  - Modern, casual restaurant
  - Stunning Bangkok skyline backdrop
  - Terrace for unparalleled city views
  - Curated cocktails
  - Signature: Steak imported from Australia and Japan, dry-aged for six weeks
- **Price Range:** Moderate to high
- **Source:** Michelin Guide

### Street Food & Local Favorites

**Thipsamai Padthai Pratoopee**
- **Location:** Bangkok's old town
- **Specialty:** Pad Thai
- **Highlights:**
  - Legendary institution perfecting Pad Thai recipe for decades
  - Must-visit for authentic Thai street food
- **Price Range:** Very affordable (street food)
- **Source:** Travel blogs, Michelin Guide mentions

**Raan Jay Fai** (see One Star section above)
- Michelin-starred street food vendor

**Wang Lang Market**
- **Location:** Bangkok Noi district (next to Chao Phraya River)
- **Highlights:**
  - Daily market
  - Known for southern food restaurants
  - Variety of spicy dishes
- **Price Range:** Very affordable
- **Source:** Wikipedia, travel guides

### Trendy Neighborhoods & Areas

**Ari**
- **Highlights:**
  - Trendy area popular among younger generations
  - Lined with stylish cafés, bakeries, and modern restaurants
  - Vibrant spot for food enthusiasts
- **Source:** Travel guides

**Chinatown (Yaowarat Road)**
- **Highlights:**
  - Bustling street food scene (one of the best in Bangkok)
  - Neon signs, gold shops, vibrant atmosphere
  - Multiple street food vendors and restaurants
- **Source:** Travel guides, video content

## Practical Information

### Reservations
- **Advanced booking recommended** for most fine dining restaurants
- **Jay Fai** - Often has long queues (arrive early or book in advance if possible)
- **La Table de Tee** - Advanced booking recommended
- Many Michelin-starred restaurants require reservations weeks or months in advance

### Best Times to Visit
- **Street food markets:** Evenings (5 PM onwards) for best atmosphere
- **Fine dining:** Dinner reservations typically 6-9 PM
- **Riverfront restaurants:** Sunset time for best views

### Dress Codes
- **Fine dining restaurants:** Smart casual to formal (check individual restaurant requirements)
- **Street food:** Casual (no dress code)

### Payment
- **Fine dining:** Credit cards typically accepted
- **Street food:** Cash preferred (carry smaller bills)
