## Executive Summary

Jumia Technologies AG (NYSE: JMIA), often referred to as the "Amazon of Africa," is a leading pan-African e-commerce platform operating across nine African countries. Founded in 2012 by former McKinsey consultants Jérémy Hodara and Sacha Poignonnec, the company became the first African tech company to list on the New York Stock Exchange in 2019. As of December 30, 2025, JMIA stock is trading at $11.62 per share.

The company operates a three-pronged business model: a marketplace connecting sellers with consumers, a logistics service (Jumia Logistics), and a payment service (JumiaPay). Despite operating in markets representing approximately 650 million people and 70% of Africa's GDP, Jumia has faced significant challenges including limited internet penetration, underdeveloped logistics infrastructure, and persistent losses.

Recent Q2 2025 results show promising signs of improvement, with revenue growing 25% year-over-year to $45.6 million and operating losses decreasing 18% to $16.5 million. However, the company continues to burn cash and faces increasing competition from local players and potential international entrants. Management maintains its goal of achieving breakeven by Q4 2026 and full-year profitability in 2027.

## Findings by Topic

### Company Overview and Business Model

**Overview:**
Jumia Technologies AG is headquartered in Lagos, Nigeria, and operates across nine African countries: Nigeria, Egypt, Kenya, Morocco, Ivory Coast, Ghana, Senegal, Uganda, and Tunisia. The company serves a combined population of approximately 650 million people, representing nearly 70% of Africa's GDP and internet users.

**Business Model Components:**

1. **Marketplace:** Connects sellers with consumers, offering products including electronics, fashion, home goods, and fast-moving consumer goods. As of 2024, the platform had approximately 70,000 active sellers and 5.4 million active consumers, processing over 22 million orders annually.

2. **Jumia Logistics:** Facilitates delivery through a network of leased warehouses, pickup stations, drop-off locations, and partnerships with over 400 local third-party logistics providers. The logistics network is managed through proprietary technology, data, and processes.

3. **JumiaPay:** Payment service designed to offer secure and convenient online transactions. In May 2024, Jumia Nigeria launched "Buy Now, Pay Later" partnerships with Easybuy and CredPal. The company plans to expand into additional financial services in the future.

**Key Metrics (2024 Full Year):**
- Annual Active Consumers: 5.4 million
- Annual Active Sellers: ~70,000
- Annual Orders: 22.7 million
- Gross Merchandise Value (GMV): $720.6 million
